Welcome,

The 30th Meeting of the EURO Working Group on Locational Analysis will take place in Jerez de la Frontera (Spain) from 17 to 19 September 2025. This periodic meeting of EURO Working Group on Locational Analysis (EWGLA) gathers researchers focused on Locational Analysis and its applications.

This year the meeting will be celebrated jointly with the XIV International Workshop on Locational Analysis and Related Problems (IWOLOCA). This activity is organized by the Spanish Network on Location and Related Problems (REDLOCA) and GELOCA, the Spanish Operations Research Society working group on Location.

The EWGLA group holds meetings every 1.5 years. Anyone interested in presenting their work, participating in the discussions, or simply learning about subjects related with locational analysis may join the group. The IWOLOCA workshops are organized annually to promote communication among REDLOCA and GELOCA members and international researchers. The last meeting took place in Granada (Spain) in September 2024.

EWGLA Sessions

  • Burcu Balçık (Özyeğin University)
    • Talk: Location and Transportation Problems in Humanitarian Logistics: Models, Insights, and Future Directions
    • Bio: Professor of Industrial Engineering and Academic Director of the Sustainability Platform. Her research spans humanitarian logistics, disaster management, food security, and healthcare resilience.
  • Anthony Papavasiliou (National Technical University of Athens)
    • Talk: An EU-Wide Electricity Generation Capacity Expansion Problem subject to Network Constraints
    • Bio: Assistant professor at NTUA, formerly at CORE (UCLouvain). Recipient of an ERC Starting Grant and the Bodossaki Foundation Distinguished Young Scientist award.

IWOLOCA Session

  • Vladimir Marianov (Pontificia Universidad Católica de Chile)
    • Talk: Celebrating Thirty EWGLA Meetings: A personal retrospective on Location Analysis
    • Bio: Professor Emeritus and former Deputy Director of the Complex Engineering Systems Institute. Expert in emergency system design and competitive facility location.
